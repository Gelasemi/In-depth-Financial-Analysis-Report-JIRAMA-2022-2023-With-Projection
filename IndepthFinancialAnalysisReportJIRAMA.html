13. Institutional and Financial Reforms

To become a driver of development, JIRAMA must undergo deep structural reforms.

A. Institutional Reform: Towards Management Autonomy

  • Legal Status: Transform JIRAMA into a State-Owned Company or a mixed-economy company, with capital open to strategic private investors.
  • Governance: Establish an independent Board of Directors, composed of experts, civil society representatives, and the state (minority).
  • Regulation: Create an independent regulatory authority (like France's CRE) to set tariffs, control service quality, and arbitrate disputes.
  • Unbundling: Legally separate the activities of Generation (open to competition), Transmission (regulated natural monopoly), and Distribution (regulated monopoly).

B. Financial Reform: Ensuring Economic Viability

  • Tariff Structure: Adopt a cost-reflective tariff, with consumption tiers and a social tariff for the poorest, financed by a targeted state subsidy.
  • Funding Diversification: Raise funds through Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs), issuing green bonds, and attracting private investment in renewable energy generation.
  • Transparency: Adopt International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) to ensure transparency and credibility with financial partners.
